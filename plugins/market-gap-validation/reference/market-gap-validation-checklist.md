# Market Gap Validation Checklist

## The Rule

No one-pager gets written until every section below is completed. No exceptions. If we can't pass all gates, we kill the idea and move on.

---

## GATE 1: Does the problem actually exist?

- Can we find **3+ independent authoritative sources** confirming the pain point? (Government reports, industry bodies, peer-reviewed research — not blog posts or vendor marketing)
- Is there **quantified dollar impact** of the problem? (Cost to businesses, lost revenue, fines, wasted hours — actual numbers, not vibes)
- Are **real people complaining** about this problem in forums, surveys, or interviews? (Reddit, industry Facebook groups, conference talks, Royal Commission transcripts, survey data)
- Is the problem **getting worse, not better**? (New regulation, growing demand, rising costs, increasing complexity — a tailwind, not a static situation)
- Is there a **forcing function** that makes solving this urgent NOW? (Regulatory deadline, program change, industry shift, seasonal pressure)

---

## GATE 2: Who exactly is the buyer?

- Can we **name the specific job title** of the person who would buy this? (Not "farmers" — which farmers? The owner? The agronomist? The contractor? The farm manager?)
- How many of these buyers exist in Australia? **(Exact number with source)**
- What do they currently spend on solving this problem? (Even if it's $0 on software — what's the cost in time, errors, fines, agency fees?)
- Can we reach them through a **specific channel**? (Industry association, conference, Facebook group, agronomist network, dealer network — not "digital marketing")
- Would they pay **$X/month** for a solution? (Gut feel is not evidence — look for pricing of adjacent tools they already pay for)

---

## GATE 3: What do they use today?

This is the gate we skipped. Never again.

- Search **"[problem] app Australia"** — what comes up?
- Search **"[problem] software Australia"** — what comes up?
- **Search the App Store and Google Play** for relevant keywords — what's already there? How many downloads? What are the reviews saying?
- **Search Capterra, GetApp, G2, and Software Advice** filtered to Australia for the relevant category
- Search **"[industry] management software Australia competitors"**
- Search **"[biggest player] alternatives"** — this surfaces the entire competitive landscape in one query
- Check **Product Hunt, Crunchbase, and LinkedIn** for startups in this space
- **List every competitor found** with: name, country of origin, founding year, pricing, key features, funding raised, estimated users/revenue
- For each competitor, answer: **does it do the specific thing we're proposing?** Not adjacent features — the actual core workflow we identified

---

## GATE 4: Where is the actual gap?

Only after Gate 3 is complete can we answer this honestly.

- Is there a **segment of buyers** that existing tools don't serve? (Too small, too regional, wrong industry sub-type, wrong price point)
- Is there a **specific workflow** within the problem that no tool handles well? (Not "they all suck" — which exact step is broken or missing?)
- Is there a **technology shift** that makes a new approach possible? (AI, new API, new data source, mobile-first, offline-first — something that didn't exist when incumbents were built)
- Is there a **regulatory or program change** so recent that incumbents haven't adapted yet? (New law, new funding model, new reporting requirement)
- Are existing tools **priced out of reach** for the target segment? (Enterprise pricing for a small-business problem)
- Are existing tools **too complex** for the target user? (Feature bloat when the buyer wants one thing done well)
- Can we articulate the gap in **one sentence** without using the words "better", "simpler", or "cheaper"? (If we can't, the gap probably isn't real)

---

## GATE 5: Can we actually build it?

- Can **two engineers ship an MVP in 10-12 weeks**? (If not, scope is too big)
- Are the **key data sources** available and accessible? (APIs, public databases, government data — confirm they actually exist and are usable, don't assume)
- Does the solution require **domain expertise we don't have**? (If yes, can we get a domain advisor before building?)
- Does it work **offline**? (If the target user is in regional/rural areas, this is non-negotiable)
- Is there a **clear first version** that delivers value without needing integrations, partnerships, or marketplace effects?

---

## GATE 6: Will it make money?

- What's the **realistic price point**? (Based on what this buyer already pays for adjacent tools, not what we think it's worth)
- How many paying customers do we need for **$1M ARR**? (Is that number plausible given the total market size?)
- Is this a **one-time sale or recurring revenue**? (Recurring = good. One-time = need volume)
- Is the buyer the **user**? (If the person using it daily isn't the person paying, sales cycle gets harder)
- What's the **expansion path**? (Can we go from one workflow to adjacent workflows? From one segment to adjacent segments? From Australia to NZ/similar markets?)

---

## Process: How We Use This

1. **Identify a pain point** — research the problem, sources, dollar impact (Gate 1)
2. **Define the buyer** — specific person, count them, find them (Gate 2)
3. **Map the competitive landscape IMMEDIATELY** — before writing anything (Gate 3)
4. **Identify the specific gap** — not "we'll be better" but "we'll do X that nobody does" (Gate 4)
5. **Confirm buildability** — data sources, technical feasibility, team capacity (Gate 5)
6. **Model the revenue** — pricing, volume, expansion (Gate 6)
7. **Only then** write the one-pager

If any gate fails, we don't proceed. We document why it failed and move to the next idea. Speed of killing bad ideas is more valuable than speed of validating good ones.

---

## Red Flags That Should Kill an Idea Immediately

- **"There are lots of competitors but they all suck"** — if 8 companies can't solve it, maybe the problem isn't solvable with software
- **"Farmers/providers/operators just need to be educated"** — if adoption is the problem, more software won't fix it
- **"We'll be simpler"** — every competitor's landing page already says "simple" and "easy to use"
- **"The market is huge"** — huge markets attract huge competitors with huge budgets
- **"Nobody is doing this"** — either we haven't looked hard enough, or there's a reason nobody is doing it
- **We can't find a single person online complaining about this specific problem** — the problem might not be real enough to pay for
